Our Story

Rooted in community.
Named for a hero.

Kunya Primary School was the first to be built — a foundation laid by the Kochar Community Based Organisation for the children of Uyoma. When those students began to excel and qualify for secondary education, the nearest options were simply too far away.

So the community built again. The secondary school was named after Ramogi Achieng Oneko — Kenyan freedom fighter and son of this very community — a name that carries the weight of struggle, dignity, and the belief that education is liberation.

"The boarding facility means no longer have to choose between household duties and their studies. They can work at night, focus, and imagine a future."

— Prof. Olola Oneko, School Board Member

Both schools are public. All teachers are employed and paid by the Kenyan government. The community's role is to build the infrastructure that makes learning possible.

What's Needed

Every gap here is a student who deserves more.

The community has identified eight priority infrastructure needs. Each one directly affects how many students can learn, stay safe, and thrive.

💧
Safe Drinking Water
The borehole pump has been repaired as a temporary fix. A permanent, reliable water source is urgently needed for 700 students and 50 staff.
🏫
10 New Classrooms
Classrooms across both the primary and secondary schools are overcrowded. Ten additional rooms are needed to meet the student population.
🔬
3 Science Laboratories
Physics, chemistry, and biology labs — critical for Kenya's national curriculum and for students aiming at university-level STEM paths.
💻
2 Computer Rooms
One for primary, one for secondary. These rooms will also serve as libraries — giving students access to digital learning tools and books.
🚻
Sanitation Facilities
4 new toilets for , plus renovation of 4 teachers' toilets. Safe, private sanitation directly affects girls' attendance and retention.
🍽️
Kitchen, Hall & Dining
A permanent kitchen with storage, replacing the current temporary structure, plus a hall for dining, concerts, and community gatherings.
🏠
8 Teachers' Houses
Housing on or near the school campus helps attract and retain qualified teachers in this rural community.
🌽
School Feeding Programme
A daily nutrition programme to combat malnutrition and keep children attending — because hungry students cannot learn.
🏗️
Already Done
4 toilets + bathroom for the boys' boarding hostel — completed by the community. The borehole pump has been repaired. Progress is real.
